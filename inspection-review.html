The moment

A 40-page report, a tight closing window, and a lot of scary-sounding language.

Inspectors are trained to flag everything. That’s the job. But a long report doesn’t mean a bad house — and a short repair list doesn’t mean a safe one. What you need is someone who builds homes for a living to tell you which items are real, which are routine, and what the actual fix costs in today’s market.

Urgent vs. cosmetic

A cracked drain line is not the same as chipped paint. We separate structural, safety, and mechanical from the nice-to-haves so you know where to focus.

Real repair costs

Not Google guesses. Ranges based on decades of running crews and writing bids — so you know what’s reasonable and what’s inflated.

Negotiation leverage

A prioritized list of what to ask the seller to repair, credit, or lower the price on — with reasoning you can hand to your agent.

Why it matters

AI can summarize your report. It can’t walk a jobsite.

AI tools can pull keywords and generate a tidy bullet list. What they can’t do is look at a photo of a cracked beam and know whether it’s a settling issue or a $30,000 structural problem. They can’t tell you that the 30-year roof the report flagged has eight good years left. They haven’t stood in a crawlspace, priced a rewire, or negotiated with a seller’s agent.

We have. For decades. That’s the difference.
